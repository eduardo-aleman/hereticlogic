## What is this?

A personal reading project spanning political philosophy, economic history, classical antiquity, urban theory, queer sexuality, and the long history of doubt. The reviews here focus on a single metric: the internal quality of the argument. I am indifferent to the author's reputation, the prevailing consensus, or whether the conclusions are comfortable.

The work on queer sexuality sits within queer studies but with a historical orientation — less interested in identity as a contemporary category than in how desire, deviance, and their regulation have been organised across time. Foucault is the unavoidable starting point, but the project extends into the ancient world, into legal and medical history, and into the contested relationship between queer theory and historical method.

Heretic Logic is a disposition, not a programme. Logic applied without deference to received authority tends to produce heresy. That seems like a reasonable starting point.
